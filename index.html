Green Tax — New Consolidated Levy

Para 7(ग)
What is the Green Tax? The budget consolidates several scattered levies that were previously collected at the customs point into a single "Green Tax" (हरित कर). The levies being merged include: Prior Development Tax (पूर्वाधार विकास कर), Road Maintenance Surcharge (सडक मर्मत दस्तुर), Road Improvement Surcharge (सडक सुधार दस्तुर), and similar charges. Instead of importers having to calculate and pay these separately, there is now one single Green Tax. This is a structural simplification — it does not necessarily mean a tax reduction, but reduces compliance complexity.

Hydropower — Private Sector International Energy Trade

Para 20
This is a Major New Policy — Private Sector Can Now Export Power Para 20 of the budget speech states: "Private sector will be given arrangements to trade electricity in international markets." Previously, only the state utility (NEA) could engage in cross-border electricity trade. This opens international energy trade to private hydropower producers — a significant policy shift.

Legal provisions to be put in place. New right

Hydropower — Permit Expiry & Government Ownership Transfer

Para 20
Forest Land & Tree Felling Fees — Adjusted at End of Permit When a hydropower project's licence/permit expires and ownership transfers to the Government of Nepal, the forest land use fees and tree felling charges that were paid during the project's operational life will be adjusted/offset against the transfer value. This prevents double-charging — promoters won't be required to pay both historical forest fees and a full transfer premium. This is a policy that incentivises long-term private investment in hydropower.

Tax Dispute Settlement — Special Scheme

Para 7(ज), 7(झ)
Two-Tier Dispute Resolution The budget introduces two separate dispute resolution mechanisms:
  • Administrative scheme (para 7ज): Long-standing tax disputes created by legal ambiguities or taxpayer ignorance — a special tax amnesty/concession scheme to be launched to resolve these and clear the backlog. Details on applicable taxes and discount rates to be in the Finance Act.
  • Court / judicial body disputes (para 7झ): For tax disputes already before courts or judicial bodies (from either side — government or taxpayer): if the taxpayer pays the determined tax amount plus a 1% surcharge within the stipulated deadline after withdrawing the case, all penalties, fines, surcharges, delay charges, and interest will be completely waived. A powerful incentive for dispute clearance.
Impact of Court Case Tax Settlement The 1% surcharge + waiver of all penalties scheme for judicial disputes is significant. If a taxpayer has a large disputed tax amount sitting in court with years of accumulated interest and penalties (which can easily double or triple the principal), settling by paying principal + 1% and getting all interest/penalties waived is a major financial incentive. Expected to clear a large backlog of outstanding tax litigation.

Local Government Taxation — New Rules

Para 70(छ)
Multiple Local Government Taxes on Goods in Transit — PROHIBITED A significant new rule: when goods are transported from one location to another passing through multiple local governments (municipalities/rural municipalities), only ONE local government may levy taxes such as transit levies (निकासी), scrap fees (कवाडी), and similar charges. Previously goods could be taxed by each local government they passed through. This is a major reform to reduce the cascading tax burden on inter-district trade and logistics.
  • PAN mandatory for provincial and local tax payments: Taxpayers paying provincial and local government taxes must open/use a Permanent Account Number (PAN). This integrates local tax compliance with the national tax ID system.

Non-Tax Revenue Changes

Para 67(घ), 69(च)
  • Umbrella law for non-tax revenue: A comprehensive umbrella legislation to be introduced to make non-tax revenue mobilisation more effective. Currently non-tax revenues (fees, charges, royalties) are governed by dozens of separate acts.
  • Cost-recovery and ability-to-pay basis: Non-tax revenues to be set on cost-recovery and ability-to-pay principles — ensuring fees reflect actual service costs.
  • Petroleum royalty from Dailekh: Petroleum commercial production in Dailekh to proceed; royalty arrangements to follow.
  • Hydropower royalties: Forest land use fees and tree-felling charges to be offset against transfer value when hydro project ownership transfers to GoN — reduces double charging.
